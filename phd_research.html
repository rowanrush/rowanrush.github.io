Cultural Geographies of LGBTQ+ Entrepreneurship in Scotland

About the Research

This research is a collaborative PhD project between the University of Edinburgh and the Community Interest Company Somewhere. Funded by the Arts and Humanities Research Council, the principle objective of the project is to carry out a social network analysis of LGBTQ+ cultural entrepreneurs in Scotland. By gaining a picture of the current networks connecting LGBTQ+ cultural entrepreneurs, Somewhere can use this information to work to increase the connectedness of LGBTQ+ entrepreneurs and reducing isolation and providing sources of mutual support. The primary research questions are

  • What and where are the spaces of LGBTQ+ cultural entrepreneurship in Scotland?
  • Which industries are LGBTQ+ cultural entrepreneurs working in?
  • What networks enable and further LGBTQ+ cultural entrepreneurship in Scotland?
  • What affordances do time-limited events give LGBTQ+ cultural entrepreneurship in Scotland and how do these compare to the affordances given by more permanent places?

My research is regularly documented in the Enterprise section of the Somewhere: For Us magazine.

I am currently in my second year of research and recruiting particpants who are willing to partake in oral history interviews or focus group discussions.

This research will be conducted primarily in-person, however Interview participants will be offered a choice in interview locations, and an online option, to ensure the greatest level of comfort. Participatory mapping workshops will take place in a private area of an appropriate LGBTQ+ supportive community space.
